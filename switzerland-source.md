# Switzerland - Source Material (Wikipedia)

## Geography & Location
- Landlocked country at intersection of Central, Western, and Southern Europe
- Bordered by Germany (north), France (west), Austria and Liechtenstein (east), Italy (south)
- Divided among Swiss Alps, Swiss Plateau, and Jura Mountains
- Alps cover most of the territory
- Population: ~9 million, concentrated on the plateau
- Major cities: Zurich, Geneva, Basel, Bern, Lausanne, Winterthur, Lucerne

## Political Structure
- Federal republic with 26 cantons
- Bern is the federal city (de facto capital)
- Four official languages: German (62%), French (23%), Italian (8%), Romansh (0.5%)
- Known for direct democracy and federalism
- Federal Council (7 members) serves as collective head of state
- Current President: Guy Parmelin (2025)

## History

### Ancient Times
- Oldest hominid traces: ~150,000 years ago
- Oldest farming settlements: ~5300 BC (Gächlingen)
- Celtic tribes: Helvetii on Swiss Plateau, Rhaetians in east
- 58 BC: Helvetii defeated by Julius Caesar at Battle of Bibracte
- 15 BC: Romans conquer Alps under Tiberius and Drusus
- Roman era: Part of Gallia Belgica, Germania Superior, and Raetia provinces
- ~260 AD: Germanic invasions, Roman towns decline

### Old Swiss Confederacy (1291-1798)
- Founded: 1 August 1291 (Federal Charter of 1291)
- Started as defensive and commercial alliance
- Expanded despite external threats and internal religious strife
- Peace of Westphalia (1648): Swiss independence formally recognized
- Remained network of self-governing states

### Napoleonic Era (1798-1815)
- 1798: Revolutionary France invades, imposes Helvetic Republic
- 1803: Napoleon abolishes republic, reinstates confederation (Act of Mediation)

### Federal State (1848-present)
- 1848: New constitution establishes federal system
- Principles: individual rights, separation of powers, parliamentary bicameralism
- Armed neutrality since 16th century
- No international war since 1815
- Joined Council of Europe (1964), UN (2002)

## International Relations
- Birthplace of Red Cross
- Hosts headquarters of WTO, WHO, ILO, FIFA, WEF, UN offices
- Founding member of EFTA
- Participates in European single market and Schengen Area
- NOT EU member

## Economy
- Highest nominal wealth per adult globally
- 8th highest GDP per capita
- Strong banking and financial sector
- Advanced pharmaceutical and biotechnology industries
- Watchmaking and precision engineering tradition
- Known for chocolate and cheese production
- Well-developed tourism industry

## Key Facts
- Area: 41,291 km²
- Population: ~9 million
- Currency: Swiss franc (CHF)
- HDI: 0.970 (2nd highest globally)
- Gini: 31.5 (medium inequality)
- Motto: "Unus pro omnibus, omnes pro uno" (One for all, all for one)
