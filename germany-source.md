## Overview

Germany, officially the Federal Republic of Germany, is a country in Western and Central Europe. It lies between the Baltic Sea and the North Sea to the north with the Alps to the south. Its sixteen constituent states have a total population of over 82 million, making it the most populous member state of the European Union.

**Neighbors:** Denmark to the north; Poland and the Czech Republic to the east; Austria and Switzerland to the south; and France, Luxembourg, Belgium, and the Netherlands to the west.

**Capital:** Berlin
**Largest urban area:** Ruhr
**Population:** ~83.5 million (2025 estimate)
**Area:** 357,022 km²

## Key Historical Events

### Ancient and Medieval Period
- Settlement since Lower Paleolithic; Neanderthal discovered in Neander Valley
- Germanic tribes emerged from Jastorf culture during Nordic Bronze Age
- Roman expansion into Germania; famous Battle of Teutoburg Forest (9 AD) stopped Roman conquest
- Holy Roman Empire formed in 962 from Kingdom of Germany
- Protestant Reformation began 1517 with Martin Luther

### Unification and Empire (1871-1918)
- German Empire established 1871 under Prussian leadership
- Otto von Bismarck as first Chancellor
- Colonial expansion in Africa and Pacific
- World War I defeat led to Weimar Republic

### Weimar Republic and Nazi Era (1919-1945)
- Democratic Weimar Republic established 1919
- Great Depression caused economic crisis
- Nazi Party rose to power 1933; Hitler became Chancellor
- World War II started with invasion of Poland (September 1, 1939)
- Holocaust: systematic murder of 6 million Jews and millions of others
- Germany surrendered May 8, 1945

### Division and Reunification (1945-1990)
- Divided into West Germany (FRG) and East Germany (GDR) in 1949
- Berlin Wall built 1961, became symbol of Cold War
- Fall of Berlin Wall November 1989
- Reunification October 3, 1990

### Modern Germany (1990-present)
- Founding member of European Union
- Euro adopted 1999
- Angela Merkel served as Chancellor 2005-2021 (first female chancellor)
- Friedrich Merz became Chancellor in 2025

## Current Political Situation

- **Government:** Federal parliamentary republic
- **President:** Frank-Walter Steinmeier (since 2017)
- **Chancellor:** Friedrich Merz (since 2025)
- **Legislature:** Bundestag (lower house) and Bundesrat (upper house)
- **16 states (Länder):** Each with own constitution and government

**Major parties:**
- Christian Democratic Union (CDU)
- Social Democratic Party (SPD)
- Free Democratic Party (FDP)
- Alliance 90/The Greens
- The Left
- Alternative for Germany (AfD)

## Economy

- **Largest economy in Europe** by nominal GDP
- **3rd largest in the world** by nominal GDP
- **6th largest** by GDP (PPP)
- Major industries: automotive, machinery, chemicals, electronics
- Strong export economy (3rd largest exporter globally)
- GDP per capita: ~$60,000 (nominal)

## Regional Context and Relations

**European Union:** Founding member, key driver of European integration
**NATO:** Member since 1955
**Neighbors:**
- France: Historical rivalry, now close EU partners
- Poland: Complex history, now EU and NATO partner
- Austria: Shared language and history
- Switzerland: Economic ties, non-EU neighbor
- Czech Republic: Strong economic ties

**Current issues:**
- Energy transition (Energiewende) from nuclear and coal to renewables
- Migration and integration
- Aging population
- Russia-Ukraine war: major supporter of Ukraine, increased defense spending
